DISCUSSION ITEMS (Kev)

Discussion A: Aus Equity Manager Selection

Aus Eagle has been the main culprit of underperformance over this period - medium and longer term analysis still attractive and consistent.

Schroders as active core is not working - its expensive beta, and uncompensated excess vol. Suggest we work on replacement?

Discussion B: Int Equity Manager Selection

GQG has been brilliant (+38% yoy), but has seen our selection alpha being concentrated to GQG and its comparative large weight. Suggest we reduce and add a second active alpha to the mix. In principal, I like Munro as currently would give us a similar allocation focus to US Technology as GQG, but would reduce our specific look forward decision risk from one manager. Suggest we indentify preferred 2nd manager.

No major issues with other managers. Laggard manager (GSF6910AU - Man GLG Asia) is a function of being our Asian allocation manager that has seen beta headwinds

With Japanese markets pulling back to their March levels, could be opportunity to add specific allocation. Either way need preferred manager; Suggest Mish you look IJP ETF or Platinum Asia (already on Wattle APL)??

Discussion C: Alternatives Growth/Defensive Characteristics

Just prior to launch all our limited-liquidity Private Equity allocations were removed by the platform on basis of liquidity concerns. As a consequence our Alternatives sleeve from a bottom-up has run less 'Growth' profile assets than was originally envisioned in our SAA analysis. The portfolio has a good variety of differentiated strategies, however too many are 'defensive' in return and risk profile. Our 70% growth SAA allocation currently funds the alternatives allocation from Growth assets. Suggest instead we at least fund half the alternatives allocation from defensives as a 'default' SAA decision - this would see increase in equities and reduction in duration & floating

Discussion D: Real Assets Volatility

Decomposition of risk shows Real Assets being the major contributor to our above Reference Portfolio realised risk measures. In aggregate, we have delivered solid risk-adjusted return from real assets since their Nov 2023 lows. However, I note this asset class has repeatedly delivered realised volatility and drawdowns that are worse than both Australian and Global equity sleeves. The main culprit to uncompensated risk over recent period has been RARE Infrastructure Income. Alceon gives us some exposure to smoothed private assets which has helped us, however it is noted we are running above (non-industry fund) peer allocation to (high vol liquid) Real Assets which over time risk eating into our Sharpe/Calmar ratios.

Alceon is our sole private asset manager. Do we increase its allocation - or do we reduce the allocation to the Real Asset sleeve on the basis that clients hold side-pocket private property/infrastructure opportunities outside the fence of the SMA?
