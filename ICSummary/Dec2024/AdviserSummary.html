Active vs Bench vs Peers - by Sector Sleeve

Beyond the previously provided standard APL paper, we have sought to provide enhanced transparency of our current Active holdings and Bench managers for each sleeve. The below provides return and risk adjusted outcomes compared to relevant peer category managers not included in the APL

Orange dots represent Active products being used in the Sector Sleeve, Light Blue dots represent Bench managers, whilst the smaller dark blue dots represent peer managers outside the APL

The centre rectangle captures 2 standard Deviations (or approximately 95%) of the datapoints, providing clarity around both positive and negative outliers.

The Y-axis shows risk adjusted returns over 3 years, the X-axis shows returns above/below our defined sector sleeve benchmark. In short top-right is GOOD, bottom-left is BAD.

It is noted that dots are only shown for products that have at least 3 years of track record - we are working on a 2nd set of analysis that will capture all products with 1 year of data.

The database of 3,500x products are categorised by Morningstar category. We have aggregated these categories to try fit within our main sector sleeve categories, however there are products (such as Talaria) that do not map well given difference in treatment between Morningstar and oursleves. This is a work in progress and we will try improve alignment.

The below charts of interactive - you can hover for more info on the dot - you can drag a box to zoom in on an area - there is also a navigation tool bar in top right corner of each chart

Generally speaking, most sector sleeves look reasonable on the basis of peer products selected vs the broader universe of peers, analysis of shorter timeframes will enhance coverage particularly for the Alternatives sleeve where many products are quite young.

Please note....given the thousands of datapoints - the charts can take a few seconds to render on your screen
